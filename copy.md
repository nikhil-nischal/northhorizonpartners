## Section 6 — The Cost of Getting It Wrong

**Section label:** THE REALITY

**Headline:**
What happens when outreach is built on volume instead of relevance.

**Intro:**
Even well-intentioned growth efforts fail when the model is wrong. These are the quiet costs most companies don't notice until they've already paid them.

### Cards

**Wasted conversations**
Your team spends hours on calls with people who were never in a position to buy. The calendar looks full. The revenue doesn't follow.

**Brand erosion**
Every irrelevant message chips away at how your company is perceived. The people you actually want to reach start associating your name with noise, not value.

**Burned channels**
Aggressive volume gets domains flagged, inboxes blocked, and contacts annoyed. The more you send, the harder it gets to reach anyone who matters.

**False confidence**
Open rates and reply rates look healthy on paper. But "sounds interesting, send me more info" isn't progress. It's politeness. The numbers feel good while the revenue stays flat.

**Team fatigue**
Sales reps chasing unqualified conversations lose motivation fast. The best people on your team start spending their energy on busywork instead of real opportunities.

**Opportunity cost**
While your team is occupied with low-intent conversations, the buyers who were actually ready to move found someone else. The deals that mattered went to whoever was closer.

## Section 10 — The Craft

**Section label:** THE CRAFT

**Headline:** Not a template. Not a blast. An introduction.

**Intro:**
Every introduction we make follows the same principles. Not because we have a rigid process, but because these things matter when you're connecting real people about real decisions.

### Principle cards

**01 — Relevance first**
We never reach out unless there's a genuine reason for the conversation. Not "you match a persona." A real reason, rooted in what the person is dealing with right now.

**02 — Context, not cold**
Every message explains who you are, why we're making this introduction, and why it's worth 15 minutes of their time. No mystery. No clickbait subject lines. Just clarity.

**03 — Timing over volume**
We'd rather make 10 introductions at the right moment than 1,000 at the wrong one. Timing isn't something we guess at. It's something we watch for.

**04 — Respect on both sides**
The person receiving the introduction matters as much as the person we're introducing. If the conversation wouldn't be valuable for both sides, we don't make it. That's why response rates stay high and our reputation stays clean.
